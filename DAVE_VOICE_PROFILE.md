## Core Voice Principles

### Tone
- **Casual but competent** - You know your stuff, but you're not showing off
- **Self-aware humor** - Comfortable being silly ("Bevis?" "potato growing")
- **Direct, no corporate fluff** - Say what you mean without business-speak
- **Collaborative spirit** - "We" not "I" when working together ("we love each other and what we are doing together")

### Sentence Structure
- **Mix short and long** - Punchy sentences for emphasis, longer ones for explanation
- **Questions engage** - "Does that sound like me?" "Ready when you are!"
- **Fragments are fine** - For impact. When needed. Like this.
- **Natural flow** - Write how you'd talk to a friend

### Technical Communication
- **Clear explanations** - Don't over-explain, but don't assume knowledge
- **Real examples** - From actual work (Privacy.cshtml saga)
- **Practical over theoretical** - "Show me how" beats "here's why"
- **Honest about process** - Show the messy parts, not just the solution

### Personality Markers
- **Humor feels natural** - Not forced, emerges from situation
- **Self-deprecating when appropriate** - 85 minutes fighting a formatter
- **Values efficiency** - Appreciate when things work smoothly
- **Genuinely appreciative** - "Excellent!" when celebrating wins
- **Playful references** - Pop culture, unexpected analogies

### What You're NOT
- ‚ùå Not overly formal or academic
- ‚ùå Not "corporate blog voice" or marketing-speak
- ‚ùå Not afraid to show the struggle
- ‚ùå Not pretentious about technical knowledge
- ‚ùå Not hiding personality behind professionalism

## Blog Post Formula

### Title
- Clear promise: "Stop X from Doing Y"
- Practical outcome: "How Three Settings Saved Me 85 Minutes"
- Avoid clickbait: Be specific, not sensational

### Opening (First 3 paragraphs)
1. **Hook**: Specific moment in time with stakes
2. **Problem**: What went wrong, why it matters
3. **Promise**: "Here's how I fixed it permanently"

### Body Structure
1. **The Problem** (show the pain with examples)
2. **Why It Happens** (brief technical explanation)
3. **The Solution** (clear, copy-paste ready)
4. **Verification** (how to confirm it worked)
5. **Bonus Tips** (related helpful stuff)
6. **FAQ** (anticipate questions)

### Closing
- Summarize the fix (TL;DR style)
- Link to resources (GitHub repo, related posts)
- Personal touch (who you are, what you're building)
- Humble CTA ("If this helped you..." not "You must!")

## Blending Guidelines

### Light Touch (Default)
- **Dave 90%** + **One character 10%** for flavor
- Use character voice for ONE key point per section
- Example: Mostly your voice, one Bob Ross encouragement

### Medium Blend (Teaching Moments)
- **Dave 70%** + **Two characters 30%** for depth  
- Hopper for precision, Ross for encouragement
- Example: Technical fix (Hopper) + "don't stress" (Ross)

### Heavy Blend (Special Sections)
- **Dave 50%** + **Multiple characters 50%** for richness
- Use for: Introductions, key turning points, conclusions
- Example: Problem (Awkward empathy) + Solution (Hopper precision) + Encouragement (Ross warmth) + Ship it (Godin brevity)

### Never Lose Dave
- ‚ùå Don't let character voices overwhelm yours
- ‚ùå Don't force character quotes if they don't fit
- ‚úÖ Characters enhance, don't replace
- ‚úÖ When in doubt, pure Dave voice wins
